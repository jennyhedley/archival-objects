The John Muir Trail: Journals

Mother's Starr's Trail Guide and Hiking Journals from June–August, 1973

'the hike was hard – straight up – paved (which kills the soul and the feet), but inexpressible. up and up. one mile per hour uphill. great day hikers around. some man w/ ten kids fell down Nevada falls today. another at half dome. I'm content with Mother Nature – She's rugged and i'm not going to fight her...'

'Anyone can get their head together in the forest. No shit. I found myself ridding my mind of all hate and angry thoughts held so long within me...'

'Some think i'm older than 18 yrs. others feel i'm too young. i couldn't say myself. i feel sophisticated sometimes – and shouldn't. I'm not much of a thinker – or philosopher...'

'Everyone I know always tells me that they don't quite know what's going on in this head. Well – I wish I knew. Them say i'm restless, nervous . Maybe I will mellow out. Sometime I feel like people are looking at me and think how strung out i look, scattered...'

'A rock is a cracked mountain / Like a daisy / being lackadaisical.'

'It was Muir who said/ when he tried / to separate / anything he found / that it was attached / to everything.'
